Drawing with graphite and charcoal pencils

Drawing using a graphite-based tool housed in a wooden case is called a pencil drawing. It might be a finished piece of art, an exercise in visual expression, or a sketch for a more detailed piece in another medium. The earlier, metallic drawing stylus that late mediaeval and Renaissance artists and craftsmen used to sketch or write on paper, parchment, or wood was replaced by the cylindrical graphite pencil because of its effectiveness in making linear, grey-black strokes with ease.
